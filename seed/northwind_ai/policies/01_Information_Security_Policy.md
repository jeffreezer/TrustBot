## 1. Purpose

This Information Security Policy is the umbrella document for Northwind AI's information security program. It establishes management's commitment to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of customer data and company systems, and it references the supporting domain policies that implement it.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all Northwind employees, contractors, systems, and the production environment supporting the Northwind Assistant and Northwind API services across Google Cloud Platform (primary) and Amazon Web Services (secondary).

## 3. Policy

- Northwind maintains a documented information security program aligned to SOC 2 (Security, Availability, Confidentiality, Privacy) and ISO/IEC 27001, 27017, 27018, and 27701.
- Security is a shared responsibility owned by a dedicated Security and Governance, Risk & Compliance (GRC) organization led by the CISO, who reports to the CTO.
- All security policies are reviewed and approved at least annually and communicated to personnel.
- Customer data is treated as confidential and protected per the supporting domain policies referenced below.
- Risks are assessed at least annually and tracked to remediation (see Risk Management Policy).

## 4. Supporting Policies

Access Control; Data Classification & Handling; Data Retention & Deletion; Cryptography & Key Management; Incident Response; Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery; Vendor / Third-Party Risk Management; Secure Software Development; Vulnerability Management; Acceptable Use; Human Resources Security; Change Management; Logging & Monitoring; Risk Management; Privacy & Data Protection.

## 5. Roles & Responsibilities

- **CISO:** owns the security program and this policy.
- **Head of GRC:** maintains policies, risk assessments, and compliance.
- **All personnel:** comply with security policies and complete required training.

## 6. Enforcement & Exceptions

Violations may result in disciplinary action. Exceptions require documented risk acceptance approved by the CISO or Head of GRC.
