> **Related controls:** CC7.3, CC7.4

## 1. Purpose

Define how Northwind detects, responds to, and recovers from security incidents.

## 2. Scope

All security events affecting Northwind systems or customer data.

## 3. Severity Levels

- **SEV-1** — confirmed breach or major customer-impacting incident.
- **SEV-2** — significant security event, contained or limited impact.
- **SEV-3** — minor event or near-miss.

## 4. Response Phases

1. **Detect** — events surface via 24/7 monitoring, alerting, and reports.
2. **Triage** — the on-call security lead classifies severity per this plan.
3. **Contain & eradicate** — isolate affected systems, remove the threat.
4. **Recover** — restore services and validate integrity.
5. **Notify** — affected customers are notified per contract/SLA; regulators as legally required.
6. **Post-incident review** — root-cause analysis and corrective actions for SEV-1/SEV-2.

## 5. Testing

The plan is tested at least **annually** via tabletop exercise; findings feed back into the plan.

## 6. Roles & Responsibilities

- **Incident Commander (CISO or delegate):** leads response.
- **On-call security:** 24/7 detection and triage.
- **Communications/Legal:** customer and regulatory notification.

## 7. Exceptions

None; deviations during an active incident are documented in the post-incident review.
